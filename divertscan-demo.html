THE PROBLEM

Most LEED diversion data is reconciled the week before submittal — in a spreadsheet, by hand, by someone who didn't capture the original tickets.

That's how 87% diversion turns into "we think it's around 87% but we can't prove it." DivertScan exists because LEED submittals deserve receipts.

01 / Capture loss

Tickets disappear in the field.

Paper tickets get crumpled, dropped, or lost. Hauler portals don't talk to each other. By submittal week, you're missing 10–15% of your weights.

02 / Math drift

Spreadsheet errors compound.

Manual entry, copy-paste mistakes, formula breaks, and inconsistent units mean the diversion % you submit doesn't match the source data anymore.

03 / No defense

"Trust me" doesn't hold up.

When an auditor asks how you got to 87%, your spreadsheet can't prove the numbers came from real scale tickets. Audit risk is permanent.

HOW IT WORKS

Three stages. One audit trail. Zero spreadsheets.

A trained human signs off on every record. The platform makes their job 10× faster.

Stage 01 · Capture

Read the scale.

A Raspberry Pi captures weights directly from your WI-127, Avery Weigh-Tronix, Rice Lake, or Cardinal indicator. iPad PWA reads tickets via GPT-4o Vision OCR. Buffered locally — works without cellular.

  • RS-232 / Ethernet · 24/7 capture
  • OCR on handwritten, dot-matrix, thermal
  • EXIF time-matching of debris photos
Stage 02 · Verify

Sign every record.

A trained human verifier reviews the source materials, sets the final diversion values, and signs off. No record reaches "verified" status without human approval. SHA-256 hash chained to the prior entry.

  • Named verifier on every approval
  • Edits create new records, never overwrite
  • Cryptographic chain of custody
Stage 03 · Report

Submit with confidence.

Generate the full submittal package for any LEED version in seconds. PDF, XLSX, JSON, plus a SHA-256 manifest the reviewer can verify in one command. Drop straight into LEED Online.

  • Diversion narrative, auto-generated
  • Embodied carbon (CO₂e) breakdown
  • Hash manifest for GBCI review

COMMON QUESTIONS

The questions every sustainability consultant asks first.

Don't see yours? Call Robert directly: (214) 507-8899

What counts as an "active project"?
A project under construction with tickets being captured. Once a project completes its LEED submittal and goes inactive, billing stops — but the data is preserved with its full hash chain.
Can I switch tiers mid-project?
Yes. Upgrade or downgrade any time. Pro-rated charges. No commitment beyond the current month on monthly plans.
What happens to my data if I cancel?
You get a full export (PDF + XLSX + JSON + SHA-256 manifest). Your records remain auditable forever — that's what the hash chain is for.
Does my scale need anything special?
Just an RS-232 serial output or Ethernet port. Works with WI-127, Avery Weigh-Tronix, Rice Lake, Cardinal, and most other indicators. We ship and configure the Pi.
How long is onboarding?
About 2 hours. We connect to your scale, import your driver list, configure projects, and walk through one full ticket capture together.
Is the AI making compliance decisions?
No. AI handles OCR and suggests a starting debris composition. A trained human verifier reviews the source materials and sets the final values that go into your LEED record.
How is this different from Green Badger?
Green Badger is upload-and-track. DivertScan starts at the scale itself — the Pi captures the weight directly, and the hash chain runs unbroken from there to submittal. Different category, not a competing feature set.
Can I migrate existing project data?
Yes. CSV import for spreadsheets, Green Badger exports, or Topia data. Imported records get their own hash manifest so you can verify the migration was clean.
